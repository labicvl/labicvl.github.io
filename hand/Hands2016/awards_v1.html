The School of Mechanical and Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue University is happy to support up to 22 travel awards of $600 each for students to travel to the HANDS workshop at the CVPR 2016*. These grants will be on a cost reimbursement basis so submission of receipts will be required. Participants may contact Michelle Sarault at msarault@purdue.edu for instructions for completing the requirements for the travel award. Please obtain a short letter from your academic advisor and send it along with a short note of your interest in the HANDS workshop to Michelle Sarault. The awards will be made on a first come basis until the maximum support possible is reached. These awards are made available through funding from the National Science Foundation CMMI division through the award: Affordable Design through Realistic Interactions for Virtual Explorations award to Professor Karthik Ramani at Purdue University.

Other conditions: Everyone that receives a grant will be required to complete a Payee Certification form. They will need to complete Section A and B and sign in Section C. They will need to return the form along with their receipts for the amount of the travel grant. Additionally, if they are not a US Citizen or Permanent Resident Michelle will set them up with an account and students will have to complete some tax information.

* Grants per applicant may increase if the successful applications are less than 22.
